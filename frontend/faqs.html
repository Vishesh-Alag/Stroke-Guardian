Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Brain Stroke Prediction System?

The Brain Stroke Prediction System is an educational tool that demonstrates how machine learning models can predict the risk of a brain stroke based on common health metrics.

How can I use the system?

You can input your age, blood pressure, cholesterol level, and heart rate on the web-based interface. The system will then predict whether you are at "High Risk" or "Low Risk" of experiencing a brain stroke.

Is the system accurate?

The system provides predictions based on available data and machine learning models. While it can offer valuable insights, it should not be considered a medical diagnosis. Consult a healthcare professional for medical advice.

How do I create an account?

To create an account, visit the Signup page and fill out the required information. Once registered, you can log in and access the system.

Can I trust the recommendations?

The recommendations provided by the system are generated based on the input data and machine learning algorithms. While they can be informative, always consult with a healthcare professional for personalized guidance.

Is my data secure?

We take data privacy seriously. Your information is stored securely and used solely for the purpose of providing stroke risk predictions. You can review our Privacy Policy for more details.

What browsers are supported?

The system is compatible with modern web browsers such as Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. Make sure your browser is up to date for the best experience.

Is this system free to use?

Yes, the system is available for educational purposes and is free to use. It is not intended for commercial use or as a substitute for professional medical advice.

Can I share my results with a doctor?

Yes, you can share your assessment results with a healthcare professional for further evaluation and discussion. It may help them better understand your health status.

How often should I use the system?

The frequency of using the system is up to you. Some users check their stroke risk periodically, while others may use it as a one-time assessment. It's essential to prioritize regular health check-ups with a healthcare provider.
